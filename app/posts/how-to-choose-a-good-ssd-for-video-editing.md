Video editing workloads put more stress on storage than many everyday tasks. Projects can include large media files, frequent reads and writes, and complex timelines that need smooth access to data. For that reason, the storage choice matters more than it does for simple office use or basic gaming.

Capacity is the first thing to think about. Large project files and cached media can grow quickly, so a drive that feels adequate today may become cramped sooner than expected. A larger SSD is often the smarter choice if you work with substantial footage regularly.

Speed is important, but it should be considered in context. High throughput helps with loading and transferring large files, but sustained performance and reliability matter just as much. If you are building a workstation around video work, a drive that balances speed, capacity, and durability is the best option.
