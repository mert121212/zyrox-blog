A power supply can be one of the hardest components to diagnose because its symptoms often overlap with other hardware issues. If the system is unstable, randomly shuts down, or shows strange behavior under load, the PSU deserves serious attention. But there is a point where continued troubleshooting stops being productive and replacement becomes the smarter move.

If the unit is old, has a poor reputation, or has already shown signs of electrical instability, it is usually better to replace it than to keep testing around it. Power supplies are not just another part; they influence the reliability of the entire system. A failing PSU can damage other components if it is left unchecked.

When the symptoms are consistent and the unit is already suspect, replacement is often the most efficient and safest path. It is one of those upgrades that protects the rest of the system as much as it improves reliability.
