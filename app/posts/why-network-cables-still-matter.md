Wireless networking is convenient, but it is not always the best option. A wired connection still offers lower latency, better consistency, and less interference than Wi-Fi in many situations. For gaming, video calls, large file transfers, or anything that depends on stable bandwidth, Ethernet can be the smarter choice.

The benefit is especially clear when the Wi-Fi signal has to travel through walls, appliances, or crowded airwaves. A cable removes much of that unpredictability. It is also easier to troubleshoot when something goes wrong because the path is simpler and more obvious.

The best network setup is usually a mix of both. Use Wi-Fi where flexibility matters and Ethernet where performance and stability matter most. That balance keeps the system practical without sacrificing quality.
