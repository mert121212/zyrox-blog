Power supply spec sheets are full of numbers, but most of them are there to make a mediocre unit look more impressive than it is. A high wattage number does not automatically mean a good PSU. The important details are usually the quality of the components, the ripple performance, the protection features, and whether the unit has been tested under real load conditions.

I have seen people buy a flashy 850W unit and then wonder why their system is unstable under load. In many cases the problem was not the wattage. It was the cheap internals, poor transient response, or a weak protection design. If you want a power supply that behaves well over time, read past the marketing and look for consistency.

A decent PSU should feel boring in the right way. It should deliver stable power without drama, without coil whine, and without turning every shutdown into a mystery.
