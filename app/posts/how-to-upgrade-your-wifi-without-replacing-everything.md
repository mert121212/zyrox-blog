A weak wireless network is often caused by placement or interference rather than the need for a completely new system. Before buying a replacement router or mesh kit, it is worth checking whether a better position, less interference, or a different channel can make a meaningful difference.

Router placement matters more than many people realize. Placing the device in a cabinet, near thick walls, or close to other electronics can create dead zones and unstable performance. Moving the router to a central and open position often improves coverage without any hardware change at all.

If the network still feels weak, upgrading a single access point or adjusting settings such as channel selection can be a more sensible step than replacing everything. The best improvement usually comes from matching the network setup to the actual environment.
