Testing a power supply does not always require professional equipment. In many cases, a careful inspection and a few simple observations are enough to flag a serious problem. The first step is visual. Burn marks, bulging capacitors, a cheap-looking build, or a very weak warranty can tell you a lot before you ever turn the system on.

If the unit is already installed, listen for unusual noises and watch whether the system behaves normally during startup. A power supply that causes random reboots, shutoffs, or inconsistent behavior under load deserves extra attention. The problem may also come from the motherboard or GPU, but PSU issues are common enough that they should be considered early.

The biggest lesson is that a PSU should be judged by consistency. A unit that delivers power smoothly and quietly is more valuable than one that offers a high wattage number on the box but behaves unpredictably. That is why protection features and build quality matter more than flashy marketing claims.
