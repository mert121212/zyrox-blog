Random reboots are frustrating because they often happen without warning and can be caused by several different problems. The first thing to do is separate hardware and software causes. If the system restarts only under load, overheating or unstable power is more likely. If it restarts even during idle, software, drivers, or a failing component may be involved.

Temperature monitoring is a good starting point. A CPU or GPU that spikes into unsafe ranges can trigger automatic shutdowns or restarts. Checking cooling, dust buildup, and fan behavior can quickly reveal whether thermals are part of the problem.

Power delivery is another major suspect. A weak or failing power supply can cause instability that looks like a software issue. If the rebooting started after a component change or an upgrade, reviewing the power supply and power connections is especially important.
