A hard drive rarely fails without giving some warning signs. The trouble is that most people do not notice them until the machine becomes unreliable. Slow file access, odd clicking sounds, delayed booting, and random freezes can all point to a drive that is beginning to fail. These symptoms are not always dramatic, but they are worth taking seriously.

The best habit is to watch for changes rather than waiting for a complete failure. If a system that used to boot quickly suddenly takes much longer, or files start to corrupt, that is a sign to back up important data right away. A failing drive can become worse very quickly. Once the problem is obvious, the time to save files is before the system becomes unstable.

Regular checks and backups matter more than any one diagnostic tool. Even a simple backup schedule can prevent a major headache. If the drive appears to be failing, the most important action is to stop relying on it for important files and move data to a safer location as soon as possible.
