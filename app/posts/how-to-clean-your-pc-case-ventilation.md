Dust builds up gradually and often goes unnoticed until the system starts running hotter or louder. The ventilation areas of a PC case are especially affected because they are where airflow is supposed to move through the machine. A blocked intake or exhaust point can quietly reduce performance and raise component temperatures.

A careful cleaning session is usually enough. Remove dust from filters, fans, vents, and the surrounding frame with gentle pressure and the right tools. Avoid blowing dust deeper into the case, and make sure the system is powered off before you start.

The goal is not to make the machine look perfect. It is to restore healthy airflow so the components can stay cooler and more stable over time. That is one of the easiest high-impact maintenance tasks you can do.
