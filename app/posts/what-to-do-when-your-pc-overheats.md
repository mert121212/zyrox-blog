Overheating is one of the clearest signs that a system is not operating the way it should. It can lead to throttling, crashes, and long-term component wear if it is left unaddressed. The important thing is to treat it as a symptom and work backward to find the cause rather than only reacting to the temperature number.

Start with airflow. Dust, blocked filters, poor case placement, and failing fans can quickly push a machine into a hotter state. If the case is full of dust or the fans are not moving air efficiently, the fix may be simple and immediate.

The next step is to check whether the issue appears under load or during idle. If it only happens during heavy tasks, the CPU or GPU cooler may be the main culprit. If the system runs hot even when doing very little, the problem may be a fan issue, poor thermal paste application, or a blocked ventilation path.
