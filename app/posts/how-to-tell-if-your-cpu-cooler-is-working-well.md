A CPU cooler is easy to overlook until the system starts feeling unstable or running hot. The good news is that there are several simple signs that can tell you whether your cooler is doing its job properly. Temperature readings, noise behavior, and system stability all provide clues.

If your CPU is idling much hotter than expected or spikes to uncomfortable levels under light tasks, the cooler may not be mounted properly or the fan may not be spinning normally. A healthy cooling setup should keep the CPU within a reasonable range and should not be noisily overworking itself when the system is not under heavy load.

Thermal paste quality and mounting pressure also matter. A cooler that is slightly loose or unevenly mounted can underperform dramatically. If the system started running warmer after a recent build or upgrade, checking the cooler installation is often the first step.
