Random reboots are one of the most annoying problems a PC can have because they feel unpredictable and usually happen at the worst possible moment. The system might crash during a game, while compiling code, or right after waking from sleep. The causes can be hardware, firmware, or software, and that is why a sloppy diagnosis usually wastes time.

The first thing I check is temperature. If the CPU or GPU gets too hot, the system may shut down or reboot to protect itself. That is especially common in builds with poor airflow or dusty filters. The next thing I look at is power delivery. A weak or failing PSU can cause instability that looks like a software issue until you test it properly.

After that, I look at drivers and BIOS state. A bad driver update, a broken overclock, or outdated firmware can create strange reboot behavior that is easy to misattribute. If the machine is unstable even at stock settings, the problem is often more basic than people expect. In that case, I go back to the fundamentals before I start changing software settings.
